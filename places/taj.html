Tajmahal

  • Location: The Taj Mahal is located in Agra, Uttar Pradesh, India, on the right bank of the Yamuna River.
  • Construction: Construction began in 1632 and was completed in 1653, taking around 21 years to build. It was commissioned by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.
  • Architecture: The Taj Mahal is renowned for its stunning Mughal architecture, combining elements from Islamic, Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles.
  • Material: The mausoleum is built primarily of white marble, which is adorned with intricate inlay work of semi-precious stones, such as jade, crystal, and lapis lazuli.
  • Dome: The central dome rises to about 35 meters (115 feet) high and is surrounded by four smaller domes. It’s a key feature of the building’s architecture.
